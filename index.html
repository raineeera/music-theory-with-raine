Introduction

“Any tone can succeed any other tone, any tone can sound simultaneously with any other tone or tones, and any group of tones can be followed by any other group of tones, just as any degree of tension or nuance can occur in any medium under any kind of stress or duration. Successful projection will depend upon the contextual and formal conditions that prevail, and upon the skill and soul of the composer” - Twentieth-Century Harmony by Vincent Persichetti.

Music has no rules, none at all. It is genuinely your open playground with complete freedom, choice, and its own special kind of anarchy. Any notes or chords can follow anything and it is up to you, the composer/musician. However, this being said, there are frameworks and concepts to build off of to make music and analyze it! With this, you can better your musical ideas, possibilities, understanding of your instruments, develop a greater apprehension and appreciation for your craft and music. I hope you join me in this adventure and it expands your world and musical journey.

There are a few expectations and a bit of upfront knowledge before we dive in. Firstly; hi, hello, hiya, I 'm Raine/Eera (you can call me either), I'm an 18-year-old who is an obsessive music nerd of extreme diversity in genre, musician, mixer, teacher, etc. I play guitar as my main instrument and have since I was 9. Bass is my second which I am confident in and love to bits. And...piano which I adore but am a novice in comparison at the moment. To get a good picture of me, I recommend you listen to my music, it is on Spotify and Bandcamp.

  • Spotify
  • Bandcamp

Second, this book will be focused/shown on guitar and bass - giving tab, for now, but it applies to everyone regardless of your instrument and these concepts stretch and extend to all of music. Who knows maybe there will be updates for sheet music and other instruments covered here!

And last, there will be assignments of sorts; Tasks that I give and that will really solidify what you learn. These practices are crucially important and I implore you to do them. They will be fun, engaging, and open your palate for expression. Tons of examples of tab and audio will be given along the way too :)
